This document specifies a DID Method, did:webs, that is both web-based and highly secure. Like its predecessor, did:web, the did:webs method uses websites and ubiquitous, mature tooling to publish DIDs and make them discoverable. Unlike did:web, this method's trust is not rooted in DNS, webmasters, X509, and certificate authorities.

The did:webs method does not need blockchains for trust; conceptually, its closest cousins are did:key and did:peer. However, it can use arbitrary blockchains as an extra, optional publication mechanism. This creates an interoperability bridge from (or between) blockchain ecosystems. The method is cheap, easy to implement, and scalable. However, it is not particularly private, and it doesn't work if parties can't use web technology. It strongly decentralizes cryptographic trust, but only weakly decentralizes publication and discovery.
